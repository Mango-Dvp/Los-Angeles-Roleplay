General Questions

What is Los Angeles Roleplay?

Los Angeles Roleplay (LARP) is a professional and immersive roleplay community based on the Emergency Response: Liberty County (ER:LC) game on Roblox. We aim to provide the most authentic Los Angeles-based roleplay experience with realistic departments, procedures, and community interactions.

Who runs Los Angeles Roleplay?

Los Angeles Roleplay is owned and operated by Sittin, who oversees all community operations, development, and management. The community is built with a focus on quality, professionalism, and immersive roleplay experiences.

Is Los Angeles Roleplay free to join?

Yes! Los Angeles Roleplay is completely free to join and participate in. We believe in creating an accessible community for all roleplay enthusiasts.

Getting Started

How do I join Los Angeles Roleplay?

To join Los Angeles Roleplay:

  1. Join our Discord server using the link on our website
  2. Complete the verification process
  3. Read and acknowledge all server rules
  4. Start participating in roleplay scenarios
What departments are available?

We have several departments:

  • Los Angeles Police Department (LAPD)
  • Los Angeles Fire Department (LAFD)
  • Los Angeles EMS
  • California Department of Public Safety
Do I need experience to join?

No prior experience is required! We welcome roleplayers of all experience levels. Our community provides resources and guidance to help new members learn and grow.

Rules & Guidelines

What is the Zero Tolerance Policy?

Our Zero Tolerance Policy means we strictly enforce all server rules without exceptions. This includes:

  • Automatic bans for RDM/VDM offenses
  • Permanent bans for hate speech or discrimination
  • Immediate action against trolling or harassment
What are the consequences for breaking rules?

Consequences depend on severity:

  • Minor offenses: Warning or temporary suspension
  • Moderate offenses: Temporary ban
  • Serious offenses: Extended or permanent ban
Can I appeal a ban or suspension?

Yes, you can appeal through our official process. However, permanent bans for severe offenses are typically not appealable.
